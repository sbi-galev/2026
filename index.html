Simulation Based Inference for Galaxy Evolution 2026

The third installment in our series of meetings bringing together experts in galaxy evolution and simulation based inference (SBI) techniques, to discuss challenges, share insights and present the latest practical tools for applying SBI to problems in galaxy evolution.

Submit abstract here
Dates: 23rd - 26th June, 2026 Location: Cambridge, UK

Context

Simulation Based Inference (SBI; see Cranmer et al. 2020) is increasingly being applied to many astrophysical problems. Following the success of our 2024 and 2025 meetings, we aim to bring together both experts in SBI as well as galaxy evolution experts with SBI experience, to discuss problems and solutions faced when applying SBI to problems in galaxy evolution. We aim to host a relatively small, focused meeting to enable plenty of time for discussion and practical sessions.

Theory

Flavours of SBI, density estimation techniques, transfer learning, model selection, hierarchical modelling, domain adaptation.

Just some of the topics covered in previous meetings...

Applications

Spectro-photometric (resolved) SED fitting, AGN modelling, resolved stellar populations, dynamics, reionisation, CMB foregrounds, population level modelling...

Tools and Techniques

Differentiable programming, forward modelling frameworks, numerical simulation approaches, calibration and convergence testing.
